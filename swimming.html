SWIMMING

Swimming in the Olympics consists of multiple competitive styles including freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly, and medley.

Freestyle

Freestyle is the fastest swimming stroke, covering distances ranging from 50m to 1500m.

Backstroke

Backstroke involves an alternating arm stroke and a flutter kick, performed while lying on the back.

Butterfly

A powerful stroke involving synchronized arm movements and a dolphin kick.

Breaststroke

A slower but highly technical stroke that requires precise coordination of arm and leg movements.

Medley

A combination of four different strokes: butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke, and freestyle.

Relay

A team-based race where each swimmer completes a section using a designated stroke.
